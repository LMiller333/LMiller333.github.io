My UX manifesto

Start with the why. Ask why (at least) five times. Frame and re-frame the problem before you even start to solve it.

 

Use data to inspire design decisions. Find the right methods to answer the question at hand. Create your own methods if you have to!

 

Test your assumptions, especially the risky ones. Being wrong is a gift. It means you have learned something new. 

 

Never forget you are designing for humans. Dignifying people is more important, and more difficult, than delighting them. 

Design is powerful. It can divide or empower. I choose the latter.
